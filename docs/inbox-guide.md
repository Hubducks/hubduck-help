# Understanding Your Inbox

## Email cards

Each email in your duck inbox is shown as a card with:

- **Title** — the email subject
- **From** — who sent it
- **Summary** — AI-generated summary of the key points
- **Importance badge** — HIGH (red), MEDIUM (amber), or LOW (green)
- **Required Actions** — tasks extracted by AI, with due dates and checkboxes
- **Events** — calendar events with date, time, and location
- **Tags** — categories like `administrative`, `academic`, `student_event`

## Working with actions

### Marking actions as done

Each action item has a **checkbox**. Click it to mark the action as completed:
- The checkbox fills green with a checkmark
- The text gets a strikethrough
- A "✓ Done" label appears
- The dashboard "Open Actions" count decreases

Click the checkbox again to reopen a completed action.

### Acknowledging emails

Click **"Got it"** on any email card to acknowledge it:
- The card mutes (lower opacity, green border)
- The button changes to "✓ Got it"
- This records that you've read and understood the email
- The compliance dashboard reflects your acknowledgment

## Importance levels

| Level | What it means | Visual |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| **HIGH** | Contains urgent deadlines or compliance requirements | Red badge |
| **MEDIUM** | Standard school communication, review when convenient | Amber badge |
| **LOW** | Informational only, no action required | Green badge |

## Calendar sidebar

The calendar on the right side of the inbox highlights dates with events and actions:

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Blue | Events on this date |
| Amber | Actions due on this date |
| Purple | Both events and actions |
| Red circle | Today |

## Action icons

At the bottom of each email card:

| Icon | What it does |
|------|-------------|
| 📋 Copy | Copy the email summary to clipboard |
| 💬 Note | Add a team note (coming soon) |
| 🗑 Archive | Archive the email for all users (admin only) |
| 🛡 Policy | Create a draft policy from this email (admin only) |
| 🚩 Flag | Flag for follow-up |
| ↗ Share | Share with a colleague |
