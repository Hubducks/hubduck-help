# Policy Management

HubDuck helps schools create, publish, and track compliance on official policies that require staff acknowledgment.

## Policy lifecycle

```
DRAFT → PUBLISHED → DELIVERED → ARCHIVED
```

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Draft** | Being prepared, not visible to staff |
| **Published** | Approved and ready for delivery |
| **Delivered** | Sent to staff for acknowledgment |
| **Archived** | Completed, removed from active view |

## Creating a policy

1. Go to **Policies** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create Policy**
3. Fill in:
   - **Title** — e.g., "Child Safeguarding Statement 2026"
   - **Category** — Safeguarding, Health, Code of Conduct, etc.
   - **Description** — Summary of the policy
   - **Deadline** — When acknowledgment is required by
4. Click **Save as Draft**

## Creating a policy from an email

If you receive an email about a policy (e.g., from the Department of Education):

1. Open the email in the duck inbox
2. Click the **🛡 Policy** icon at the bottom of the card
3. HubDuck creates a draft policy pre-filled with the email content
4. Edit the title, category, and deadline as needed

## Publishing and delivering

1. When the draft is ready, click **Publish**
2. Then click **Deliver** to send it to staff
3. Choose delivery method: via the digest or immediately

## Tracking compliance

The **Policies** page shows:
- **Active Policies** — how many are currently in play
- **Overall Compliance** — percentage of staff who have acknowledged
- **Overdue Users** — who hasn't acknowledged past the deadline
- **Acknowledgment progress** per policy — green bar showing completion

## Mandatory school policies

Irish primary schools must have these policies in place:

1. **Admissions Policy** — Fair, transparent, Board-approved
2. **Child Safeguarding Statement** — Children First Act 2015 compliance
3. **Code of Behaviour** — Education (Welfare) Act requirement
4. **Anti-Bullying Policy** — DES Anti-Bullying Procedures
5. **Health & Safety Statement** — Governance Manual requirement

Department inspectors check compliance during Whole-School Evaluations.
